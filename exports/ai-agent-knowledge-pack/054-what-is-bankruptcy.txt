Customer Scenario: What is Bankruptcy?
Scenario Title: What is Bankruptcy?
Category: Application Processing & General Operations
Keywords: Bankruptcy, BO, Bankruptcy Order, Order

Answer To Give The Customer:
Bankruptcy is a legal process designed to help individuals who can’t repay their debts. It gives you a fresh financial start by writing off most of your unsecured debts, but it also comes with serious consequences and restrictions.

📝 How do you apply?
In the UK, you can apply for bankruptcy online through the government’s Insolvency Service. You don’t need to go to court unless someone else is making you bankrupt. It’s a big decision, so it’s important to get advice before applying.

💷 How much does it cost?
As of now, it costs £680 to apply for bankruptcy. This fee must be paid before your application can be submitted, although payment plans may be available in some cases.

👥 Who manages your bankruptcy?
Once your bankruptcy is approved, it is managed by an Official Receiver – a person who works for the Insolvency Service. They’ll review your finances and may take control of certain assets to help repay your debts. In some cases, a Trustee may also be appointed to handle this.

⚠️ What restrictions does bankruptcy create?
When you're bankrupt, there are several restrictions you must follow, including:

You can’t borrow more than £500 without telling the lender you’re bankrupt.

You can’t act as a company director without court permission.

You must cooperate fully with the Official Receiver and provide any information they request.

Your credit file will show the bankruptcy for 6 years, which can make getting credit, renting, or even some jobs more difficult.

Your assets may be sold, and you could be asked to make monthly payments for up to 3 years if you have disposable income.

📌 Important: Bankruptcy can be life-changing, so it’s always best to speak to a free debt adviser before applying. They can help you explore all your options and make the best choice for your situation.
