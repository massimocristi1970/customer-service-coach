Customer Scenario: What happens if a customer goes bankrupt?
Scenario Title: What happens if a customer goes bankrupt?
Category: Application Processing & General Operations
Keywords: bankruptcy process, bankruptcy order, bankruptcy, dividend, written off, WO, official reciever, proof of debt, POD, bankrupt, insolvency, insolvency services

Answer To Give The Customer:
You’ll be notified
If a customer is made bankrupt, you’ll usually receive a notice from the Official Receiver or see it on the Insolvency Register. Alterntively, the customer may make you aware directly.

Stop all contact and collection
You must stop chasing the customer for payment — no calls, emails, letters, or legal action. Bankruptcy protection means you're legally not allowed to pursue the debt.

You can submit a claim to the Official Receiver (or Trustee) showing how much you’re owed, with any evidence (like statements or contracts).

Wait for updates
The Receiver will deal with the customer’s assets. If there’s money left after priority debts and fees, you might get a small payment (called a dividend), though this is rare.

Debt is written off
After 12 months, bankruptcy usually ends. Most unsecured debts (like loans or credit cards) are written off. You can’t chase the customer after this.
