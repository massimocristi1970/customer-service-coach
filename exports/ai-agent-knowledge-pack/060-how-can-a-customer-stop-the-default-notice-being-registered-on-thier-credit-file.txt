Customer Scenario: How can a customer stop the default notice being registered on thier credit file?
Scenario Title: How can a customer stop the default notice being registered on thier credit file?
Category: Application Processing & General Operations
Keywords: default notice, default letter, stop, DFN, default, credit reference agency, CRA

Answer To Give The Customer:
To prevent the default notice from being recorded with the credit reference agencies, the customer must either:

Settle the balance stated in the letter by the date noted in the default notice. (Note: the balance in the letter may differ from the current outstanding balance, as interest has accrued since the letter was issued.)

Make a partial payment and agree for the remaining balance to be cleared either in full by a later date or through an affordable repayment arrangement.

Note: If the amount stated in the default notice is not settled in full by the deadline, interest will continue to accrue according to the new schedule and will be payable.
