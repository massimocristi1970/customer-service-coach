Customer Scenario: The customer is asking about Early Settlements.
Scenario Title: Early Settlements
Category: Application Processing & General Operations
Keywords: early, settlement, settle early, ESF, pay early

Answer To Give The Customer:
Purpose:
To explain how early settlements work and provide staff with a clear process to follow when customers request to pay their loan in full before the end of the agreed duration.

Key Points:

Customers can settle their loan early by paying:

The outstanding principal, and

The interest accrued up to the settlement date.

The settlement figure provided is valid only on the day it is quoted.

If payment is not made on the same day, the settlement figure will increase daily at a rate of 0.8% of the outstanding principal.

Advise the customer:

“This figure is valid for today only. If payment is not made today, the settlement figure will increase tomorrow by 0.8% of the outstanding principal.”

Note the account with the settlement figure given and the date.

Formula for Daily Settlement Increase:

Daily Increase = Outstanding Principal × 0.008

Example:

Outstanding principal: £1,000

Daily increase: £1,000 × 0.008 = £8.00

If the customer does not pay today, the quoted settlement figure will increase by £8.00 tomorrow.

Important Notes for Staff:

Always remind customers that early settlement saves them from paying further future interest, but the figure changes daily.

Do not attempt to fix a settlement figure beyond the day it is quoted.
