Customer Scenario: The customer is asking about Understanding & Managing Customer Vulnerabilities.
Scenario Title: Understanding & Managing Customer Vulnerabilities
Category: Application Processing & General Operations
Keywords: vulnerability, conditions, mental health, identifying, vul

Answer To Give The Customer:
Purpose:
To help staff recognise and respond appropriately when customers disclose vulnerabilities, in line with Consumer Duty and treating customers fairly.

1. Health-Related Vulnerabilities

Examples: Physical disability, serious illness, mental health conditions, addiction, cognitive impairment.

How to Respond:

Show empathy and patience.

Offer to slow down the call or provide information in writing.

If needed, place account into VUL (Vulnerability) status.

Provide signposting to specialist support (e.g. debt charities, mental health helplines).

2. Financial Vulnerabilities

Examples: Job loss, reduced income, high cost of living, reliance on benefits, sudden financial shock (e.g. bereavement, separation).

How to Respond:

Explore affordable solutions (payment plans, short-term hold).

Remind them about free, impartial debt advice services.

Record notes clearly with context.

3. Situational Vulnerabilities

Examples: Bereavement, domestic abuse, caring responsibilities, divorce/separation, homelessness.

How to Respond:

Be sensitive to emotional impact.

Avoid pressing for immediate decisions.

Offer flexibility and note the account accordingly.

Escalate if safeguarding concerns arise (e.g. risk of harm).

4. Communication Barriers

Examples: Low literacy, English not first language, hearing or speech difficulties, limited access to digital tools.

How to Respond:

Adapt communication style — speak clearly, avoid jargon.

Offer alternative formats (email instead of phone, written confirmation).

Allow extra time on calls.

Record any preferences in account notes.

5. Age-Related Vulnerabilities

Examples: Elderly customers with reduced memory/understanding, very young customers with limited financial experience.

How to Respond:

Check understanding by asking the customer to repeat back.

Provide extra explanations where needed.

Be mindful of potential safeguarding concerns.

Escalate if exploitation or financial abuse is suspected.

6. Risk of Harm / Safeguarding

Examples: Suicidal thoughts, domestic abuse, threats of violence, immediate safety risks.

How to Respond:

Stay calm and listen.

Record facts only, not opinions.

Key Staff Reminders

Never judge or dismiss what the customer discloses.

Provide reassurance and explain what support options are available.

Escalate if unsure — better to check than mishandle a vulnerable customer.
