Customer Scenario: What is a Summary of Borrowing
Scenario Title: What is a Summary of Borrowing
Category: Application Processing & General Operations
Keywords: SOB, Summary, Payday lending market, borrowing

Answer To Give The Customer:
A Summary of Borrowing (SOB) is a regulatory document a lender must provide to a customer who has taken a High-Cost Short-Term Credit (HCST) product (for example, payday or other short-term, high-cost loans). It gives a clear, concise breakdown of how much the customer has borrowed and how much they have paid in charges (interest and fees) over a reporting period.

Purpose:

Show the total amount borrowed during the reporting period.

Show the total charges (interest, fees, and other costs).

Improve transparency so customers can see the true cost of repeat or short-term borrowing.

Help customers make informed decisions about future borrowing and seek debt advice if needed.

Key points:

The SOB is informational only — it is not a collection notice or enforcement action.

It is a regulatory requirement designed to protect consumers and ensure visibility of repeated HCST borrowing.

When a customer SHOULD be sent an SOB

Every three months (quarterly), in arrears, covering the previous three-month period — regardless of whether payments are due or the account is active, where the customer has had HCST borrowing that meets the FCA reporting criteria.

When the account is closed (including where the debt is sold or transferred to another creditor/third party).

Where there has been repeat borrowing from the lender (i.e., the customer has borrowed more than once), or otherwise where the FCA’s HCST reporting criteria apply.
