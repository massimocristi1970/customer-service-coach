Customer Scenario: What are the consequences of non-payment?
Scenario Title: What are the consequences of non-payment?
Category: Collections & Account Status Management
Keywords: consequences, non-payment, non payment, failed arrangement, default notice, debt recovery, debt agency, failed payment

Answer To Give The Customer:
1. Arrangement Cancelled

If the customer misses a payment or breaks the terms of the plan:

The current payment arrangement will be cancelled.

The account will return to active collections, meaning further contact attempts may be made to resolve the arrears.

You should always tell the customer:

“If the agreed payment is not made, the arrangement may be cancelled and your account will return to collections for further action.”

2. Pending Default Notice Issued

If the account continues to remain unpaid:

A Pending Default Notice may be issued.

This is a formal warning that a default could be recorded if no action is taken.

It gives the customer an opportunity to bring the account back in line before a default is applied.

This should be explained clearly:

“If the arrears are not resolved, we may issue a Pending Default Notice which is a formal step before a default is registered with credit reference agencies.”

3. Default Registered with Credit Reference Agencies

If the customer still fails to contact us or make payments:

A default may be registered on their credit file.

This will remain on their credit report for six years.

It can affect their ability to obtain credit, mortgages, phone contracts, or other financial services.

Explain simply:

“A default can impact your credit rating for six years and may affect future borrowing.”

4. Third-Party Debt Recovery Agency Instructed

If the account cannot be resolved directly:

The debt may be transferred to a licensed third-party debt recovery agency.

The customer will then be contacted by the agency, not us.

The agency may use their own contact processes to recover the balance.

Explain clearly and calmly:

“If the account remains unpaid, it may be passed to a third-party debt recovery agency who will continue recovery efforts.”

5. Additional Collections Activity

Depending on the customer’s behaviour and account history, further action may include:

Increased outbound contact to try to engage the customer

Written correspondence outlining the arrears and next steps

Reassessment of the customer’s affordability if they re-engage

These actions should always be proportionate, fair, and compliant with regulatory requirements.

6. Potential Legal Action 

Escalation to the legal team for pre-legal assessment

The possibility of a County Court Judgment (CCJ)

Further enforcement if a CCJ remains unpaid

7. Impact on Future Lending

If the customer continues to default or shows poor repayment behaviour:

They may be restricted from future borrowing

Their account may be flagged internally

They may be declined for new applications until the debt is resolved

Explain in simple terms:

“Non-payment can affect future lending decisions with us.”

Summary to Read to Customers

Here’s a simplified version for agents to use during calls:

“If the payment isn’t made, the arrangement may be cancelled and your account will return to collections.
If it remains unpaid, we may issue a Pending Default Notice which can lead to a default being registered on your credit file for six years.
The account may also be passed to a third-party debt recovery agency.
It’s really important we work together to agree a plan that is realistic and sustainable for you.”
