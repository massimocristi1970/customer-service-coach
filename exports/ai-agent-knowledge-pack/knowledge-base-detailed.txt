Title: What is the Right to Withdraw (RTW) process?
Category: Right to Withdraw (RTW) (right-to-withdraw)
Section: Manual Entry
Source: Tick Tock Loans Knowledge Base v1
Last Updated: 2025-06-27
Keywords: right to withdraw, RTW, 14 days, funded, 30 days, daily interest, 0.8%, principal, credit file

Answer Content:
The customer can withdraw from the agreement within 14 days of the loan being funded. This period begins the day after the loan is funded. Once a customer has withdrawn, they have 30 days to repay the balance. This balance will accrue daily interest at a rate of 0.8% per day, based on the outstanding principal.
You should set a repayment date within the 30-day period via the Accounts tab in the Schedule section. Once the loan has been settled, raise a "Customer Service -- Withdrawal from Agreement" task. After full RTW compliance, the loan entry will be removed from the customer's credit file with the credit reference agencies.

Title: Right to Withdraw (RTW) Process
Category: Right to Withdraw (RTW) (right-to-withdraw)
Section: Manual Entry
Source: Manual Entry
Last Updated: 2025-06-27
Keywords: Withdraw, RTW, right to withdraw, cancellation, cancel loan, cancel agreement, opt out, 14-day rule, 14 days, fourteen days, exit agreement, cooling-off period, loan withdrawal, customer wants to cancel, agreement withdrawal, stop loan, terminate agreement, ends loan, cancels loan, wishes to withdraw, no longer wants loan

Answer Content:
If a customer wishes to withdraw from their loan agreement, they must inform us within 14 days of the loan being funded. This 14-day period starts the day after the loan is funded.
The customer can notify us of their intention to withdraw either verbally or in writing.
The customer has 30 days from the date they notify us of their intention to withdraw to repay:
The amount borrowed, plus
The daily interest accrued up to the date the repayment is made.
Ask the customer to confirm the exact date they intend to make the repayment.
Create a new payment schedule for that date.
Set yourself a follow-up task on the same date to check whether the payment was made and follow up if necessary.
If the Customer Can’t Confirm a Date schedule the repayment for the 30th day after the withdrawal notification.
If the customer is unhappy with a card payment being scheduled, switch the payment method to bank transfer to avoid automated card collection attempts.
The balance will remain due, and the customer will continue to receive payment reminders as part of the usual communication cycle. If unpaid, the account will flow into the standard collections strategy.

Title: Interest Query
Category: Transaction Responses & Payment Issues (transaction-responses)
Section: Manual Entry
Source: Manual Entry
Last Updated: 2025-06-27
Keywords: Interest, daily interest, %, percentage, interest rate, charged interest, how much interest, contractual interest, calculate interest, work out interest, interest amount, arrears interest, quote interest, interest quote, interest query, interest calculation, total interest, cost of interest, breakdown of interest, accrued interest, what is the interest, interest explained, interest charge, interest fees, why charged interest, interest on loan, interest figure, interest applied, interest query help, customer asking about interest, interest per day, how interest works

Answer Content:
Process Overview

Refer to the customer’s original loan schedule and explain the Total Amount Payable (TAP) they agreed to at the start of their loan.
Let them know that this amount reflects what they would repay if all payments were made on time, with no changes to their repayment dates.
If the TAP has increased due to changes in payment behaviour, explain why:
Interest accrues daily at a rate of 0.8% on the outstanding principal.
Any missed payments, changes in payday, or reduced payments may lead to an increase in the total interest charged.
Reassure them that interest is capped at 100% of the amount borrowed — this is a rule set by our regulator, the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA).
Help the customer understand how they can reduce their interest:

The more they pay, the less interest is charged, as the balance decreases.
Even nominal payments (small amounts) can help reduce the interest if made within the interest-charging period.
If the customer expresses frustration (e.g., “You’re ripping me off” or “You’re like loan sharks”), respond calmly and clearly:

We are a regulated lender, authorised by the FCA.
We follow all legal rules on interest and have clearly outlined the terms in the agreement the customer signed.
We do not charge more than the 100% cap, and customers have control over how much interest builds up by making timely or early payments.
Offer to help them explore ways to settle early or reduce their repayment burden.

Title: Fraud Process
Category: Fraud & Disputes (fraud-disputes)
Section: Manual Entry
Source: Manual Entry
Last Updated: 2025-06-27
Keywords: Fraud, suspected fraud, confirmed fraud, fraudulent, fraud alert, fraud team, fraud department, fraud process, report fraud, fraud claim, fraudulent transactions, unauthorised transactions, scam, scam activity, identity theft, account hacked, account compromised, fraud investigation, fraud check, fraud complaint, card blocked, card compromised, stolen card, fraudster, impersonation, police report, Police, crime report, crime reference number, CRN, action fraud, notice of correction, NOC, CIFAS, report to police, fraud escalation, fraud refund, fraud steps, fraud query, possible fraud, transaction not mine, not the customer, someone used.

Answer Content:
Process Overview:

Customer confirms that the bank details are not theirs:

If a customer contacts us to say they didn’t apply for the loan — often after receiving a default notice or spotting it on their credit file — follow these steps:
Verify Identity & Cross-Check Details
Ask the customer to confirm their full name, email address, contact number, and bank account details.
Compare these to what’s recorded on the account.
Outcome Based on Matching Details
If the email, phone number, and bank details match:
This likely indicates the customer, or someone close to them, made the application.
The customer would usually remain liable.
If you’re uncertain, escalate to the Fraud Specialist – Zoe Quick.
If any details do not match, especially the bank details:
Record the customer's correct contact details in the system for follow-up.
Advise the customer to report the loan as fraudulent to the bank used on the application.
The bank must investigate and provide a written response confirming the findings.
Hold the Account
Place the account on hold for 30 days while waiting for bank confirmation.
After 30 days or upon receipt of the bank’s response, escalate to Zoe Quick for fraud investigation.
Customer Confirms All Details Are Correct – But Denies Applying

If the customer confirms that all details on the loan are correct, but insists they didn’t apply:
Advise Immediate External Reporting
Instruct the customer to report the incident to:
Their bank
Action Fraud
The police (to obtain a crime reference number)
Request Supporting Evidence
We require a letter from the bank confirming that the loan funds were deposited and withdrawn by a third party without the customer’s consent.
This confirmation must be in writing and clearly state that the transaction was fraudulent.
Fraud Outcome Handling

Once confirmed in writing:
The customer will be expected to repay the original loan amount (principal), since the funds were received.
The business will then write off any interest that accrued.
This aligns with our policy where fraud is proven and funds are reimbursed by the bank.

Title: Breathing Space Workflow
Category: Debt Respite Scheme / Breathing Space (debt-respite)
Section: Manual Entry
Source: Manual Entry
Last Updated: 2025-06-27
Keywords: breathing space, financial hardship, vulnerable customer, vulnerability specialist, Zoe Quick, mental health, physical health, suicide, self-harm, bereavement, caregiver, income expenditure form, I&E, collections hold, 14 days, evidence request, financial vulnerability, breathing space review, chaser email, hold expired, arrears flow, on hold pending evidence, vulnerability flag, line manager referral, health conditions, hospitalization, medical treatment, family member involvement, collections activities pause, repayment plan, financial stability

Answer Content:
Step 1: Identify Eligibility for Breathing Space
Trigger Event:
•	Consider breathing space if a customer discloses financial hardship, or is identified, or discloses as vulnerable.
Initial Assessment:
•	Review the customer's circumstances to determine if breathing space is suitable. Serious vulnerabilities, particularly health or mental health concerns, should be assigned to the Vulnerability Specialist (VS) - Zoe Quick.
Triggers for Referral to  Vulnerability Specialist (VS)
Please note: If the customer displays any of the following triggers, please note the account and assign to the Vulnerability Specialist. If the customer does not display any of the triggers below, then continue to step 2
Direct Disclosure of Health Conditions
•	The customer mentions a diagnosed mental health or physical health condition (e.g., depression, anxiety, cancer) that affects their ability to manage finances or respond to communications.
•	They discuss ongoing medical treatment or challenges that may impact their financial stability.
Mentions of Suicide or Self-Harm
•	If a customer mentions suicide, self-harm, or hints at thoughts of ending their life,
References to Significant Health Events
•	They mention a recent major health event, such as surgery, hospitalisation, or a new serious diagnosis, impacting their ability to meet financial obligations.
•	Statements like “I just got out of the hospital” or “I’ve been dealing with a serious health issue” can serve as indicators.
Bereavement
•	The customer mentions experiencing the recent loss of a loved one, which could impact their emotional and financial stability.
•	Statements like “I’m struggling after the loss of [family member]” or “I’ve just lost someone close to me” are strong indicators of vulnerability.
Family or Caregiver Involvement
•	A family member or caregiver emails on behalf of the customer, explaining that the customer has health or cognitive issues affecting their ability to manage the account.
•	The customer mentions they need someone’s help due to health-related challenges, signalling potential vulnerability.
Step 2 : Implement and Monitor Breathing Space
Income and Expenditure Form:
•	Send  a copy of the income and expenditure form to the customer at the start of the 14-day hold.
•	Request evidence, where appropriate,  of any change in circumstances (e.g., job loss, reduced income).
Hold Collections Activities:
•	Pause collections activities for the initial 14-day breathing space period and move the account to a new stage labelled " On Hold - Pending Evidence”
Customer Communication:
•	Send  confirmation Email - Account on Hold for 14 Days with Request for Evidence and I&E Completion or Email - Account on Hold for 14 Days with Request to Complete I&E Only (as appropriate) to the customer confirming they are in a hold stage and set a follow-up date for the 14-day review.
Step 3: Review and Extend if Necessary
Breathing Space Review:
•	Before the end of the initial 14-day period, assess the customer’s financial situation based on the income and expenditure form and any additional evidence provided.
•	Note: If at this stage the customer is deemed to be financially vulnerable, then please refer the account to a line manager who will determine whether or not a vulnerability flag should be added to the account
Follow-Up for Unreturned Forms:
•	If the customer has not returned their income and expenditure form within the 14 days, contact them to remind them of the request, send  Chaser Email for Evidence and I&E Request or Chaser Email for I&E Completion Only (as appropriate) . Provide an additional 14 days for them to return the form. Send a second chaser email at 21 days Second Chaser Email for Evidence and I&E Request (Sent at 21 Days) or Second Chaser Email for I&E Completion Only (Sent at 21 Days)
Step 4: Closure
End Breathing Space:
•	If the customer’s financial position has improved or a repayment plan can be agreed based the income and expenditure provided by the customer conclude the breathing space period, move the account out of the " On Hold - Pending Evidence " stage, and outline next steps for resuming payments.
Unreturned Income and Expenditure:
•	If the requested completed income and expenditure form or evidence of change in circumstances is not received within the extended 14-day period, return the account to the normal collections workflow and send Email - Hold Expired, Account Returning to Arrears Flow to the customer advising that the breathing space period has ended.

Title: What is Bankruptcy?
Category: Application Processing & General Operations (application-processing)
Section: Manual Entry
Source: Manual Entry
Last Updated: 2025-08-08
Keywords: Bankruptcy, BO, Bankruptcy Order, Order

Answer Content:
Bankruptcy is a legal process designed to help individuals who can’t repay their debts. It gives you a fresh financial start by writing off most of your unsecured debts, but it also comes with serious consequences and restrictions.

📝 How do you apply?
In the UK, you can apply for bankruptcy online through the government’s Insolvency Service. You don’t need to go to court unless someone else is making you bankrupt. It’s a big decision, so it’s important to get advice before applying.

💷 How much does it cost?
As of now, it costs £680 to apply for bankruptcy. This fee must be paid before your application can be submitted, although payment plans may be available in some cases.

👥 Who manages your bankruptcy?
Once your bankruptcy is approved, it is managed by an Official Receiver – a person who works for the Insolvency Service. They’ll review your finances and may take control of certain assets to help repay your debts. In some cases, a Trustee may also be appointed to handle this.

⚠️ What restrictions does bankruptcy create?
When you're bankrupt, there are several restrictions you must follow, including:

You can’t borrow more than £500 without telling the lender you’re bankrupt.

You can’t act as a company director without court permission.

You must cooperate fully with the Official Receiver and provide any information they request.

Your credit file will show the bankruptcy for 6 years, which can make getting credit, renting, or even some jobs more difficult.

Your assets may be sold, and you could be asked to make monthly payments for up to 3 years if you have disposable income.

📌 Important: Bankruptcy can be life-changing, so it’s always best to speak to a free debt adviser before applying. They can help you explore all your options and make the best choice for your situation.

Title: What happens if a customer goes bankrupt?

Section: Manual Entry
Source: Manual Entry

Title: Debt Relief Order
Category: Collections & Account Status Management (collections-account)
Section: Manual Entry
Source: Manual Entry
Last Updated: 2025-09-18
Keywords: DRO, Relief, Debt Relief, Insolvency, debt management, order

Answer Content:
A Debt Relief Order (DRO) is a formal insolvency tool designed for individuals with low income, minimal assets, and relatively small unsecured debts who are unable to repay their creditors. DROs provide a structured mechanism for managing qualifying debts while offering a clear path to debt write-off.

Key Features:

Debt Eligibility: Unsecured debts, including credit cards, personal loans, overdrafts, utility arrears, and other qualifying debts. Current debt limit: £30,000 (England & Wales).

Eligibility Criteria:

Total qualifying debts ≤ £30,000

Limited assets (generally no more than £2,000, excluding certain essential items)

Low disposable income (typically less than £75 per month after essential outgoings)

Must not have had a DRO in the past six years

Duration: Standard DRO period is 12 months. If circumstances remain unchanged, qualifying debts are written off at the end of this period.

Credit Impact: DROs remain on the individual’s credit file for six years, affecting future credit applications.

Application Process:

Must be completed through an approved intermediary (debt adviser from an approved organisation).

A one-off fee of £90 is payable before submission.

Effect During DRO: Creditors included in the DRO cannot pursue repayment while the order is active.

Considerations for Staff:

DROs are suitable only for those meeting strict eligibility criteria; staff should verify circumstances carefully.

While DROs provide debt relief, they have long-term implications for the customer’s credit rating and financial options.

Staff should advise customers of alternative solutions if DROs are not appropriate.

Export ID: 76
Title: What is an Annual Percentage Rate (APR)
Category: Application Processing & General Operations (application-processing)
Section: Manual Entry
Source: Manual Entry
Last Updated: 2025-10-15
Keywords: APR, annual percentage, interest, total amount payable

Answer Content:
What is APR?
APR stands for Annual Percentage Rate. It represents the total cost of borrowing over a year, including interest and any compulsory fees, expressed as an annual percentage of the amount borrowed.

Why Do We Use It?
Although our products are short-term and interest is typically calculated on a daily basis, we are still required by regulation to display an APR. This ensures transparency and consistency across all lenders, allowing customers to compare the cost of credit between different products and providers — even if the borrowing period is much shorter than a year.

How It Relates to High-Cost Short-Term Credit (HCSTC):

In high-cost short-term lending, the APR appears very high because it’s an annualised figure based on daily interest rates applied to short borrowing periods.

For example, a loan that runs for 30 days with daily interest may look disproportionately high when shown as an annual rate, but this doesn’t mean the borrower is paying that amount — it’s simply how the cost is standardised for comparison.

The key figure for our customers remains the total amount repayable and the daily interest rate, which reflect the true short-term cost of borrowing.

In Summary:
We calculate interest daily to reflect short borrowing terms, but we must display the APR to comply with consumer credit regulations and maintain transparency for customers.

Title: What is a Notice of Sum in Arrears
Category: Application Processing & General Operations (application-processing)
Section: Manual Entry
Source: Manual Entry
Last Updated: 2025-11-18

Answer Content:
A Notice of Sum in Arrears is a formal letter sent by a lender/creditor when a borrower has fallen behind on their repayments for a credit agreement regulated by the Consumer Credit Act (for example: loans, credit cards, hire purchase, store cards).

The notice is issued when the borrower has missed the equivalent of two or more payments, or has underpaid to the point that the arrears reach the value of two full instalments.

Its purpose is to:

Inform the borrower that they are behind on payments

Confirm the total amount of arrears owed

Explain any consequences, such as further action if the arrears are not cleared

Provide required information and protections, including where to get free debt advice

It is a statutory notice, meaning the creditor must send it. It is not a court order and does not mean legal action has already started.

Title: What is a Summary of Borrowing
Category: Application Processing & General Operations (application-processing)
Section: Manual Entry
Source: Manual Entry
Last Updated: 2025-11-18
Keywords: SOB, Summary, Payday lending market, borrowing

Answer Content:
A Summary of Borrowing (SOB) is a regulatory document a lender must provide to a customer who has taken a High-Cost Short-Term Credit (HCST) product (for example, payday or other short-term, high-cost loans). It gives a clear, concise breakdown of how much the customer has borrowed and how much they have paid in charges (interest and fees) over a reporting period.

Purpose:

Show the total amount borrowed during the reporting period.

Show the total charges (interest, fees, and other costs).

Improve transparency so customers can see the true cost of repeat or short-term borrowing.

Help customers make informed decisions about future borrowing and seek debt advice if needed.

Key points:

The SOB is informational only — it is not a collection notice or enforcement action.

It is a regulatory requirement designed to protect consumers and ensure visibility of repeated HCST borrowing.

When a customer SHOULD be sent an SOB

A lender must send a Summary of Borrowing:

Every three months (quarterly), in arrears, covering the previous three-month period — regardless of whether payments are due or the account is active, where the customer has had HCST borrowing that meets the FCA reporting criteria.

When the account is closed (including where the debt is sold or transferred to another creditor/third party).

Where there has been repeat borrowing from the lender (i.e., the customer has borrowed more than once), or otherwise where the FCA’s HCST reporting criteria apply.

Title: What is a Subject Access Request (SAR)?
Category: Security, Verification & Data Requests (security-verification)
Section: Manual Entry
Source: Manual Entry
Last Updated: 2025-11-21
Keywords: DSAR, subject access request, SAR, data request

Answer Content:
A Subject Access Request is a request made by an individual for access to the personal data an organisation holds about them.
SARs fall under the UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018.

A SAR can be:

Written (email, letter, webform)

Verbal (phone call, in person)

Made to any staff member — not just the Data Protection Officer

No special wording is required. If a customer says anything like “I want my data”, it may be a valid SAR.

Answer Content:
A request is a SAR if the individual asks for:

“Everything you hold about me”

“All my notes, calls, emails, recordings”

“Any personal data you have on me”

“A copy of my information”

If unsure, treat it as a SAR and escalate.

Title: DSAR (Data Subject Access Request) Process
Category: Security, Verification & Data Requests (security-verification)
Section: Manual Entry
Source: Manual Entry
Last Updated: 2025-11-21
Keywords: DSAR, SAR, Process, Subject access request, data request, information request

Answer Content:
Step 1 — Acknowledge the Request

Thank the customer and confirm that we can help.

Advise that we will need to log the request for processing.

If on a call, note that the request is verbal — this is still valid.

Step 2 — Raise the Correct Task

Agents must raise a Complaint – Subject Access Request task.
This must be assigned to Alex Cross to action all DSAR responses.

This step is mandatory for every DSAR, regardless of channel or phrasing.

Step 3 — Inform the Customer of the Timescales

Provide the standard timeframe information:

We must respond within one month of receiving the request.

If the request is complex or involves large volumes of data, we may need to extend the deadline by up to two additional months.

If an extension is needed, the customer must be informed within the first month.

3. Identity Verification

Before data can be released, identity must be verified.
Follow our standard security checks.
If identity can't be safely confirmed, the timeframe is paused until verification is completed.

4. What Happens After the Task Is Raised

Handled by Alex Cross:

Logging the DSAR

Gathering all relevant data (notes, correspondence, call recordings, documents)

Reviewing and redacting where needed

Preparing the DSAR response pack

Sending securely to the customer

Closing the complaint task

Recording completion in the SAR log

Agents do not prepare, review, or send DSAR information themselves.

5. What to Avoid

Never refuse a request because it’s verbal

Never send personal data directly

Never promise specific data points (e.g., “we definitely have that call recording”)

Never delay raising the task — the one-month timeframe starts immediately upon receipt

6. Standard Script Example (Optional for Agents)

“I can help with that. I’ll log a Subject Access Request for you today. We must respond within one month, and if the request is complex we may extend by up to two additional months — but we would let you know within the first month if that’s needed.”
